Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

A key exchanged proposed in 1976, and currently used in SSL/TLS, SSH, IPSec. Both parties agree on publicly known parameters, p and alpha, and then choose a random private key. The process involves both sides taking the alpha and raising it to the exponent of their private key mod p. They then exchange values and raise the other's value to the power of their own private key mod p and that is the session key.

RSA

RSA (known as Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is a public-key cryptosystem used for secure data transmission (mainly keys), and digital signatures. They tend to have public keys with values n and e as well as private value d unique per user. To encrypt plaintext x to ciphertext y the following is done y = x^e mod n and to decrypt it's x = y^d mod n.
