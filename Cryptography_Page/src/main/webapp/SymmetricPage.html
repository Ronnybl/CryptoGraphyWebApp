Stream Cipher

A stream cipher encrypts and decrypts every bit individually. A corresponding keystream of equal size is combined and shifts the cipher/plain text to yield the product. An error will occur if the input and key are not of equal size.

RC4

RC4 (known as Rivest Cipher 4) is a stream cipher, it goes through a key scheduling process, pseudo random number generation and the key generated is then XOR'd with the plaintext to encrypt it.

BBS

Blum Blum Shub was invented by 1986 by Lenore Blum, Manuel Blum, and Michael Shub. It's a pseudorandom number generator.

DES

Stands for Data Encryption Standard, this block cipher does 16 identical operations where it uses a different derivative of the main key every round. It encrypts blocks of 64 bits at a time but a key length of 56 bit is proved to be insecure, and this cipher was later replaced by AES

*
*
   

AES

Also known as Advanced Encryption Standard. After a need for a new block cipher was announced, two Belgium cryptologists came with Rijndael and beat the other 14 candidate algorithms. Depending on key length, there could be 10, 12, or 14 rounds in the cipher process. This is proved to be safer as brute force is impossible due to the key length and it's considered quantum-safe since the cipher can adapt to a quantum attack by increasing key size
